--- layout: main title: MOTECH - Mobile Technology for Community Health ---

MOTECH addresses the challenge of evalutating a patient's history, comparing it to a prescribed schedule of care, and initiating messages between patients, caregivers, administrators, and external healthcare systems. Working with our partners, we expand the effectiveness of solutions built on MOTECH.

Development partners

Dimagi produces CommCare, a mobile phone-based job aid for community health workers which facilitates better interaction between health care workers and patients. The CommCare UI is a series of forms that walk the caregiver and patient through the information and questions to be covered for a specific appointment protocol. In addition to presenting information, CommCare can be used to input data about the patientâ€™s visit. CommCare runs on medium-low capability phones and higher end phones.

CommCareHQ is the server component which stores the data collected by the CommCare handset application. CommCareHQ communicates with MOTECH server to coordinate care between the two systems.

CommCare and MOTECH perform complementary roles with CommCare facilitating interactions between CHW and client as well as CHW workforce management while MOTECH is responsible for messaging to clients and caregivers as well as connections to external healthcare systems. CommCare and MOTECH are currently partnering on work with CARE in Bihar.

InSTEDD has several technogies which insulate application developers from the complexity of dealing with individual operator/aggregator IVR or SMS systems. Grameen is partnering with InSTEDD to connect their Nuntium and Verboice technogies to expand the reach of the SMS and IVR capabilities of MOTECH applications.

Implementation Partners

CARE is a major participant in the Ananya effort in Bihar, India. Their team is re-engineering the training and supervision of the front line health workers in that state. They are using a combination of MOTECH and CommCare to better equip these health workers, collect data on the health care they deliver, and close the loop with patients who miss their schedule care.

The BBC World Service Trust is also a major participant in the Ananya effort in Bihar, India. BBCWST is creating job aids and training materials which will be distributed to front line health workers via MOTECH.

World Health Partners runs a network of private francised health clinics in India. They will be using MOTECH to increase the range of services their clinics can perform, starting with reporting and adherence tracking of tuberculosis patients.

Johnson & Johnson is deploying the Treatment and Mobile Alerts program in urban India. This project aims to increase the appointment and treatment adherence for patients on anti-retroviral regimens.

Grameen Foundation has deployed MOTECH in the Upper East Region of Ghana. This project launched in July, 2010 and is now being scaled up nationally.
